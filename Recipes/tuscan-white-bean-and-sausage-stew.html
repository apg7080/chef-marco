Tuscan white bean and sausage stew

A cozy Tuscan one-pot that splits cleanly: two adult bowls finished with chicken sausage, kale, and a parmesan rind, plus a quiet baby bowl of creamy beans and soft carrot pulled before any salt or sausage goes in. Pantry beans do the heavy lifting, so active time stays around fifteen minutes.

03

Method

15 minutes of active time.

  1. 6 min Sweat the aromatics.

    Two tablespoons of olive oil in a heavy pot over medium. Add diced onion, carrot, and minced garlic. Cook six minutes until soft and fragrant, stirring so the garlic doesn't catch.

  2. 10 min Simmer the beans.

    Add the drained cannellini beans and four cups of low-sodium broth. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook ten minutes until the carrot is tender and the beans are creamy.

  3. 1 min Set baby portion aside.

    Spoon out about half a cup of beans and vegetables into a shallow bowl before any sausage or salt goes in. Mash the beans lightly with a fork. No salt, no sausage, no parm yet.

  4. 10 min Finish the adult pot.

    Drop the sliced chicken sausage, the parmesan rind, and the chopped kale into the pot. Simmer ten more minutes until the sausage is cooked through and the kale is silky. Season with flaky salt and cracked pepper.

04

Plate it

One safe choice in each grip category.

  • Spoon load. Pre-load a baby spoon with mashed beans and soft carrot.
  • Pinch. A few whole soft beans and small carrot dice for thumb-and-finger practice.
  • Strip grip. Steamed kale ribbon or a soft carrot baton on the side.

Beans hold heat. Test a spoonful on your wrist before serving.

Safety check

From the Chef Marco Baby Safety Notes for a 12-month-old.

Pass

  • No salt added at any step
  • No honey
  • No choke shapes: whole grape, whole blueberry, round meatball, whole nut, hard raw carrot, hard raw apple, whole cherry tomato
  • Texture is lumpy + strips
  • Only 4 teeth (front incisors), no molars yet: every piece must squish between finger and thumb, nothing that needs grinding
  • No high-mercury fish in this recipe
  • Contains common allergens: milk. Confirm none are on the no-go list for this child.
  • Sit-down eating only, never propped or in a moving stroller
06

Storage and reheat

Glass jars only. No plastic for warm food.

  • Fridge. 4 days in sealed glass.
  • Freezer. Up to 2 weeks. Thaw overnight in the fridge.
  • Reheat. Splash of broth or water, microwave thirty seconds, stir, thirty more. Wrist test.
  • Do not. Reheat the baby portion more than once. Freeze the baby portion with the adult sausage stew. Keep them separate.
07

Adult version

Same base, one extra minute, fully seasoned.

After the baby bowl is set aside, add the sliced Italian chicken sausage, a parmesan rind, and the chopped kale to the pot. Simmer ten minutes, then season hard with flaky salt and cracked black pepper. Ladle into deep bowls with a drizzle of olive oil, a dusting of grated parmesan, and torn crusty bread or grilled sourdough to mop the broth.
