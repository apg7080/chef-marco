What you are making

Homemade vanilla cake pops, dipped yellow, with a chocolate smiley face.

A from-scratch vanilla cake, crumbled and bound with a quick buttercream, rolled into balls and dipped in melted white chocolate tinted sunny yellow. A piped dark-chocolate face finishes each one. Built entirely from regular supermarket ingredients.

Ingredients

Makes about 24 cake pops. All supermarket aisles.

Coloring note: white chocolate seizes into a grainy paste if it touches water-based (liquid) food coloring. Use oil-based or gel color. If your store only carries liquid, buy pre-colored yellow candy wafers instead.
03

Method

45 minutes of active time, plus chilling.

  1. 30 min Bake a simple vanilla cake.

    Heat oven to 350°F. Cream the softened butter with granulated sugar until pale. Beat in eggs and vanilla. Whisk flour, baking powder, and salt separately, then add to the butter in three additions, alternating with the milk. Spread into a greased 8-inch pan and bake 25 to 30 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean. Cool completely.

  2. 5 min Make the buttercream binder.

    Beat the ¼ cup butter with powdered sugar, vanilla, and just enough milk to make a thick, smooth frosting. This glues the crumbs together.

  3. 10 min Crumble and mix.

    Crumble the fully cooled cake into fine crumbs with your hands. Add the buttercream a few spoonfuls at a time, mixing until the crumbs hold together when you squeeze a handful. Stop while it is still firm, not wet or oily.

  4. 20 min Roll and chill.

    Roll into roughly 1-inch balls, about 24. Set on a lined tray and chill 30 minutes in the fridge or 15 in the freezer until firm but not frozen solid.

  5. 10 min Melt and color the coating.

    Melt the white chocolate with the oil gently — microwave in 30-second bursts and stir between each, or use a double boiler. Stir in oil-based or gel yellow until evenly sunny. Do not use liquid coloring; water seizes chocolate.

  6. 5 min Attach the sticks.

    Dip about ½ inch of a stick into the melted coating, then push it halfway into a chilled ball. The coating sets like glue. Chill 5 minutes so the sticks hold.

  7. 20 min Dip them yellow.

    Submerge each pop fully in the yellow coating in one motion. Lift straight up and gently twirl, letting the excess drip back. Stand upright in a block of styrofoam or an upturned colander to set.

  8. 10 min Pipe the smiley faces.

    Melt the dark chocolate chips, spoon into a small zip-top bag, and snip a tiny corner. Once the yellow has set firm, pipe two dot eyes and a curved smile on each pop. Let the faces set before serving or packing.

04

Serve it

Faces out, sitting down.

  • Serve. Stand the finished pops in a jar or foam block so the faces show.
  • Party. Tie a thin ribbon under each face for a birthday or party tray.
  • Baby taste. For a young toddler, offer a small stick-free crumb of plain cake, supervised — never the pop on its stick.

These are a treat, not a meal. Serve sitting down.

05

Safety check

This one is a treat, not a baby meal — read the warnings.

Notes

  • No honey
  • No high-mercury fish
  • Contains added sugar — an occasional treat, recommended 24 months and up
  • On a stick: a choking hazard. Never hand a baby or young toddler the pop or the bare stick
  • Contains allergens: wheat, milk, egg, soy. Chocolate may carry trace nuts — check the label
  • Sit-down eating only, supervised
06

Storage

Airtight, room temperature, faces out.

  • Room temp. Airtight container, 2 to 3 days.
  • Fridge. Up to 7 days, covered. Let condensation evaporate before serving.
  • Freezer. Freeze undipped balls up to 4 weeks. Thaw before coating.
  • Do not. Refrigerate uncovered; condensation dulls the shine and softens the coating.
07

Dress them up

Same base, a few extra touches.

Stir a drop of almond extract into the coating, or dip half the batch in dark chocolate finished with a pinch of flaky salt. Sprinkles or edible glitter pressed on under the wet coating dress them up for a party.
