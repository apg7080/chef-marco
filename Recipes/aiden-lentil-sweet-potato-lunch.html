Lentil and sweet potato lunch for Aiden

A warm, lumpy Mediterranean mash with shredded chicken, made in one round of prep to cover three weekday lunches. Built on preps already in the rotation, no added salt at any step.

What you are making

Three weekday lunches from one round of prep.

Built on preps already in the rotation: Cooked French lentils, Roasted boneless chicken thighs. Pull these from the fridge before you start.

03

Method

25 minutes of active time.

  1. 12 min Steam the sweet potato.

    Cubes over simmering water until fork-soft. Drain well.

  2. 2 min Warm the lentils.

    Skillet over medium with the olive oil and the pinch of cumin. No salt.

  3. 3 min Combine and mash.

    Sweet potato, lentils, shredded chicken, and yogurt in a bowl. Fork-mash to lumps.

  4. 1 min Adjust.

    Too thick: splash of water or breast milk or formula. Too loose: more sweet potato.

  5. 2 min Portion.

    Divide into three glass jars, about three-quarters of a cup each. Cool fully before sealing.

  6. 15 min at 400°F Roast the zucchini sides.

    Finger-length strips on parchment, no salt. Pull when golden and squishable.

  7. At serve Plate the tray.

    Mash in the well, zucchini and avocado and quartered berries around the rim. Loaded spoon for self-feed, strips for hand grip.

04

Plate it

One safe choice in each grip category.

  • Spoon load. Pre-load a baby spoon with mash and set it on the tray edge.
  • Strip grip. Zucchini and avocado give long shapes for the whole-hand grab.
  • Pinch. Quartered berries and small chicken shreds for thumb-and-finger practice.

Open cup with assist for water. Bib on, tray under high chair. Expect mess; that is the practice working.

05

Safety check

From the Chef Marco Baby Safety Notes for a 10-month-old.

Pass

  • No salt added at any step
  • No honey
  • No choke shapes: whole blueberry, round meatball, whole grape, whole nut, hard raw carrot, hard raw apple
  • Texture is lumpy + strips
  • No high-mercury fish in this recipe
  • Contains common allergens: milk. Confirm none are on the no-go list for this child.
  • Cumin is new; start small, watch two to three days for reactions
  • Sit-down eating only, never propped or in a moving stroller
06

Storage and reheat

Glass jars only. No plastic for warm food.

  • Fridge. 3 days in sealed glass.
  • Freezer. Up to 4 weeks. Thaw overnight in the fridge.
  • Reheat. Thirty seconds in the microwave, stir, thirty more. Test temperature on the inside of your wrist before serving.
  • Do not. Refreeze a thawed portion. Reheat more than once.
07

Adult version

Same base, one extra minute, fully seasoned.

After the three baby jars are sealed: add flaky salt, cracked pepper, a crumble of feta, a heavier drizzle of olive oil, and a squeeze of lemon. Serve warm over arugula, or alongside a torn piece of sourdough. A respectable desk lunch on the same twenty-five minutes of active time.
