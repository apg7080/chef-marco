Boursin eggs on toast with avocado and tomato

A fifteen-minute breakfast worth getting out of bed for. The baby's third comes out of the pan before any Boursin or salt; the rest gets the soft-fold treatment and lands on avocado toast with blistered tomatoes alongside.

Method

15 minutes of active time.

  1. 3 min Blister the tomatoes.

    Warm a teaspoon of olive oil in a nonstick pan over medium-high. Cherry tomatoes cut-side down, leave them two to three minutes until they blister and slump. Slide onto a plate. No salt.

  2. 2 min Toast the bread, mash the avocado.

    Toast goes in. Meanwhile, mash the avocado in a small bowl. Hold the salt and lemon — pull the baby's quarter first, then season.

  3. 3 min Scramble soft and slow.

    Whisk the eggs with the optional milk. Drop heat to medium-low, add olive oil, pour eggs. Pull gently from the edges. Stop while the curds are still glossy.

  4. 30 sec Pull the baby's portion.

    Scoop a third of the pan into a small bowl. This is the baby's — no Boursin, no salt. Optional: fold in a pinch of unsalted grated cheddar for a cheesy texture without the soft-ripened cheese.

  5. 1 min Fold Boursin into the adult eggs.

    Drop the Boursin in by the teaspoon, fold through with the residual heat. The cheese melts into ribbons. Salt, pepper, chili. Pull off the heat — eggs keep cooking.

  6. 2 min Build the baby plate.

    Toast finger lightly squished, soft avocado slices or plain mash alongside, plain egg curds in pinch pieces, quartered blistered tomatoes with skins broken.

  7. 2 min Build the adult plates.

    Smashed avocado on toast (salt, lemon now), Boursin eggs piled on, blistered tomatoes alongside, flaky salt and a thread of olive oil to finish.

04

Plate it

One safe choice in each grip category.

  • Pinch. Pinch-sized soft egg curds and quartered blistered tomato (skins broken) for thumb-and-finger practice.
  • Strip grip. Toast finger and soft avocado slices give long shapes for the whole-hand grab.
  • Spoon load. Pre-load a baby spoon with plain avocado mash for self-feeding.

Pull the baby's third before any Boursin or salt go in. Tomato skins are a choking shape — quarter and squish.

Safety check

From the Chef Marco Baby Safety Notes for a 10-month-old.

Pass

  • No salt added at any step
  • No honey
  • No choke shapes: whole cherry tomato, whole grape, whole nut, chunk cheese
  • Texture is soft pinch
  • No high-mercury fish in this recipe
  • Contains common allergens: egg, milk, wheat. Confirm none are on the no-go list for this child.
  • Sit-down eating only, never propped or in a moving stroller
06

Storage and reheat

Glass jars only. No plastic for warm food.

  • Fridge. 1 days in sealed glass.
  • Freezer. Up to 0 weeks. Thaw overnight in the fridge.
  • Reheat. Best fresh. Eggs and toast both lose texture fast.
  • Do not. Boursin in the baby's portion — soft-ripened style and salty. Whole cherry tomatoes — always quarter and squish skins broken.
07

Adult version

Same base, one extra minute, fully seasoned.

Once the baby plate is on the tray: Boursin folded through the eggs, salt and pepper and chili, flaky salt at the plate, a squeeze of lemon over the avocado toast. A breakfast that's worth getting out of bed for.
